Age and Enrollment Trends Across Healthcare Programs

Heidi Tam

Data Science Major at UC San Diego

Email: hetam@ucsd.edu

PURPOSE: to analyze age and enrollment trends across various healthcare programs in the United States from December 2024 - June 2025 to help policymakers, healthcare providers, and insurers make informed decisions

OBJECTIVES:

  • To monitor enrollment spikes and declines over time to judge program participation
  • To compare which healthcare providers are the most popular and observe the distribution of health programs for each provider
  • To discover which health programs are the most appealing and to use milestone achievement data as a way to evaluate how well providers support individual progress
  • To explore how various age groups are involved with enrollment and program involvement
  • To visualize how users progress over time, in terms of milestone progression and enrollment

DATA: 23,831 rows and 10 columns

COLUMNS:

  • Date of Birth: the date of birth of the individual, used to segment users by age group
  • Provider Name: the organization or company delivering a specific health service
  • Program Name: the specific service offered by the provider
  • Current Status: indicates whether the individual is enrolled in the program
  • Enrollment Date: the date the individual enrolled in the program
  • Highest Milestone: the highest milestone the individual achieved in the program
  • Gender: Male or Female
  • Race: the racial background of the individual for demographic analysis
  • LOB: line of business; a categorization of the program type
  • Age: the age of the individual, calculated from the date of birth

ANALYSIS ON ENROLLMENT OVERVIEW

  • In December 2024, the number of currently enrolled members was about 1,044. The number of enrolled members increased in January 2025 to 4,556. Enrollments then decreased until March 2025, dipping just above 3,500. Enrolled members spiked again at in April 2025 close to 5,000 before decreasing until June 2025.
  • Notable spikes at January 2025 and April 2025, but not enough data to decide if the spikes are recurring.
  • 336.4% increase in enrollments from December 2024 to January 2025.
  • 28.01% decrease in enrollments from Janurary 2025 to March 2025.
  • 259.67% decrease in enrollments from April 2025 to June 2025.
  • Overall Drop-Off Rate: 4.65%

ANALYSIS ON PROVIDER PERFORMANCE

  • Enrollments are mostly concentrated in Headspace (5,772) and Betr Health (3,795). The rest of the providers generally have less than 2,000 enrollments.
  • Providers with unusally high enrollments (Upper Outliers): Headspace (5,772), Betr (3,795), Dario Health (1,926), Sworkit Fitness (1,833)
  • Providers with unusually low enrollments (Lower Outliers): EX Program (153), Restore Health (146), Virta (40), Nymbl Science (7)
  • Headspace, the leading provider in terms of enrollments, only provides the SSR program. It is also the only provider that offers that program. Likewise, Headspace Care is the only provider that offers MH-HIGH, and Sworkit Fitness is the sole provider of MSK-LOW. Dario Health offers both HTN and DMP in nearly equal proportions. The other providers in the top 10 contain some mix of mostly NDPP with some IBC.

ANALYSIS ON PROGRAM INSIGHTS

  • The most popular programs are NDPP (6,654) and SSR (5,772). The least popular programs were FPP (7) and MCDPP (61).
  • A steep drop-off after the top programs could suggest specialized or under-marketed programs.

ANALYSIS ON DEMOGRAPHICS

  • The binned age groups of the enrolled members are slightly right-skewed, with a peak around 35-years-old indicating mostly mid-career users.
  • The middle 50% of most of the programs range from about 35 to 70 years of age, which indicates a diverse age range. However, the FPP program had a middle 50% from 67 to 80 years of age, which means they primarily targeted senior citizens.
